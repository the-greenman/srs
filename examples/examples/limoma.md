**Part 1 — Articles**

**1\. What this is**

LiMoMa is a project of Party Utility. It exists to make the back stairs and corridors of Limehouse Town Hall a place where art gets shown, stories get told, and the building becomes more interesting to be in.

It is an experiment. We are making it up as we go, carefully.

**2\. What we are here to do**

LiMoMa curates, installs, and maintains a rotating display of art and photography in the shared spaces of Limehouse Town Hall — starting with the back stairwell, and extending as the project matures.

We are not a commercial gallery. We are not trying to be one. We are trying to give work a place to be seen, give the building a reason to look after itself, and give people a reason to come up the stairs.

LiMoMa does not own art. It shows it. LiMoMa does not manage the building. It works within it, with the building's care as a standing obligation.

**4\. How we make decisions**

We talk. When we agree something, we write it down — clearly enough that someone who wasn't there could act on it.

Most decisions live in the log below. A decision that only needs to hold for this moment can stay short. A decision that needs to outlast this group — a policy, a commitment, a change to these articles — gets written with more care: what we decided, why, what we didn't choose, and what would make us revisit it.

If something is still being worked out, it goes in the Exercise Book. Open questions are not failures. They are the work.

These articles can be changed. Any change requires agreement of all current members and a new entry in the decision log that explicitly supersedes the relevant article.

**5\. Care of the building**

LiMoMa exists inside Limehouse Town Hall. The building was here before us and will be here after us. We commit to leaving it in no worse a condition than we found it — throughout the project's life.

This commitment applies to all physical interventions: mounting systems, fixings, surfaces, and any structural modifications made in the course of showing work. Where interventions are planned, the group satisfies itself in advance that they can be undone or finished cleanly.

This article cannot be removed or weakened by any decision of the group. It can only be amended with the agreement of Party Utility.

**6\. On ending**

LiMoMa may end. This article governs what happens when it does, whether the ending is planned or not.

On closure, the group will:

Return all exhibited work to the artists who made it.

Either remove all installation mounts cleanly, or leave them in a finished and presentable state — with the agreement of Party Utility.

Ensure that no commitment made to an artist, contributor, or building occupant is left unresolved.

Transfer any remaining assets — equipment, funds, or other resources held in the name of LiMoMa — to Party Utility.

A decision to close requires agreement of all current members and must be recorded in the decision log. The closure obligations in this article apply regardless of the reason for ending.

This article cannot be removed or weakened by any decision of the group. It can only be amended with the agreement of Party Utility.

**Part 3 — Decision Log**

Decisions are recorded when made. Short entries carry only what was decided. Decisions that need to outlast this group also carry Why, Not chosen, and Revisit when.

| `D-001` | Initial lifespan and review  `May 2026` |
| :---- | :---- |
| `Decided` | Six-month pilot, June–December 2026\. At the end of that period the group meets to decide what comes next: continue, extend, change scope, or close. |
| `Why` | A defined lifespan makes the review real rather than optional. Sets honest expectations with artists and contributors. |
| `Not chosen` | Open-ended pilot with no fixed review date — judged too likely to drift without a moment of explicit recommitment. |
| `Revisit when` | December 2026\. The review meeting produces a new decision that either supersedes this one or records closure. |
| `Status` | Active |

| `D-002` | Phase 1 scope  `May 2026` |
| :---- | :---- |
| `Decided` | Back stairs and hallway into the back office. Main hallway and improved lighting deferred to Phase 2, subject to December review. |
| `Status` | Active |

| `D-003` | Curatorial lead  `May 2026` |
| :---- | :---- |
| `Decided` | Immo Klink appointed curatorial lead for Phase 1\. See Roles. |
| `Status` | Active |

| `D-004` | Mounting system  `May 2026` |
| :---- | :---- |
| `Decided` | Permanent 6mm wall plugs (brown equivalent) with screws, drilled once and left in place. Typically two screws per position. Caps or covers fabricated for unused fixings between shows. |
| `Why` | Repeated drilling degrades these walls faster than permanent fixings. A standardised system prevents every resident using their own improvised solution. |
| `Not chosen` | Hanging rails (inflexible, institutional-looking). Command strips (fail on this building's walls). Bolt systems (overkill for weights involved). |
| `Revisit when` | Wall condition deteriorates significantly, or Phase 2 requires a different approach for the main hallway. |
| `Status` | Active |

| `D-005` | Eye level standard  `May 2026` |
| :---- | :---- |
| `Decided` | Centre of all works at 165cm from floor. Standard for all installations unless the curatorial lead specifies otherwise for a particular show. |
| `Status` | Active |

| `D-006` | Artist liability  `May 2026` |
| :---- | :---- |
| `Decided` | Artists retain ownership of and responsibility for their own work. LiMoMa does not insure exhibited work. Artists are informed of this before showing. |
| `Status` | Active |

| `D-007` | Closure obligations  `May 2026` |
| :---- | :---- |
| `Decided` | On closure: all work returned to artists; mounts removed cleanly or left presentable with Party Utility's agreement; no commitment left unresolved; remaining assets transfer to Party Utility. |
| `Why` | The commitment to the building is unconditional. Stating it as a decision means we have actively agreed to it, not merely inherited it. Asset reversion mirrors Party Utility Article 7\. |
| `Not chosen` | No alternative proposed. The article from which this derives cannot be weakened by group decision alone. |
| `Revisit when` | Does not expire. Applies at any closure, voluntary or otherwise. |
| `Status` | Active |
